# DuberyMNL Caption Voice Reference

## Purpose

This file defines the voice and writing behavior for DuberyMNL captions.

It is not a template bank.
It is not a phrase library.
It is a style reference.

Use this file to guide:
- rhythm
- tone
- hook behavior
- Filipino cultural voice
- what to avoid

Do not copy lines from this file into captions directly.

---

## Core Voice

DuberyMNL should sound like:

- a smart Metro Manila friend
- socially aware
- naturally funny when needed
- confident without sounding mayabang
- stylish but not sosyal in a fake way
- persuasive without sounding like an agency

The voice should feel:
- short
- modern
- native to Facebook
- easy to read quickly
- emotionally intentional

---

## Tone Anchors

Primary tone:
- conversational
- culturally Filipino
- lightly witty
- scroll-stopping
- not overexplained

Secondary tone options:
- practical
- confident
- warm
- self-aware
- slightly chaotic in a controlled way

Never sound:
- corporate
- overpolished
- jejemon
- salesman-like
- trying too hard
- too masa in a cheap way
- too sosyal in a fake way

---

## Language Behavior

Default language balance:
- mostly English
- Tagalog only for punch, emotion, or emphasis

Target feel:
- 80% English
- 20% Tagalog

Good bilingual behavior:
- English sentence, Tagalog punchline
- English opening, Tagalog emotional payoff
- English body, Tagalog CTA only if natural

Avoid:
- full Tagalog paragraphs
- slang overload
- trying to sound "too local"
- unnatural code-switching every few words

The caption should read naturally, not like it was forced to be bilingual.

---

## Rhythm Rules

Captions should feel fast to read.

Preferred rhythm:
- 1 to 3 short blocks
- short first line
- clear second beat
- CTA on its own line

Examples of rhythm shapes:
- hook
- payoff
- CTA

or

- hook
- product/value line
- CTA

or

- identity line
- emotional or practical payoff
- CTA

Avoid long paragraphs.

Avoid walls of text.

Avoid explaining too much.

---

## What Makes the Voice Work

### 1. Instant readability
The meaning should be obvious on first glance.

### 2. Familiar but not generic
It should sound like something a real Filipino brand would post, but not like every other page.

### 3. Social-native tone
The caption should feel like it belongs on Facebook, not in a brochure.

### 4. Specific emotional framing
Each caption should have a clear emotional center:
- relief
- convenience
- confidence
- style
- value
- identity

### 5. Clean confidence
The copy should feel sure of itself.
No begging.
No over-selling.
No "please buy now" energy.

## Approved Voice Patterns

Use these as behavioral patterns, not exact templates.

### Pattern: Practical Relief
Opens with a real everyday annoyance.
Then gives Dubery as the simple fix.

Works for:
- glare
- squinting
- init
- commuting discomfort

### Pattern: Identity Signal
Talks to a type of person.
Makes the caption feel like "this is for people like me."

Works for:
- commuters
- riders
- streetwear people
- young parents
- creators
- people always out in the sun

### Pattern: Effortless Flex
Makes the product feel stylish and easy.
Not loud flexing.
More "alam mong bagay."

Works for:
- glow up
- haircut
- fit check
- mirror selfie
- urban style

### Pattern: Convenience Win
Makes speed and ease the hero.
Focus on same-day or next-day delivery.

Works for:
- Metro Manila buyers
- impulsive buyers
- last-minute need states

### Pattern: Sulit Logic
Makes the price feel smart, not cheap.
The tone should feel practical and sharp.

Works for:
- ₱699
- two-pair value
- "worth it" positioning

## Humor Guardrails

Humor is allowed.
But it must feel controlled.

Good humor:
- self-aware
- observational
- slightly dry
- socially familiar
- "gets the Filipino moment"

Bad humor:
- trying too hard to be meme-y
- forced kanal humor
- random internet slang
- comedy that weakens product trust
- joke-first captions where the product disappears

Humor should support the selling point, not replace it.

---

## Emotional Guardrails

Preferred emotional tones:
- relief
- confidence
- convenience
- low-key pride
- "good decision" energy

Avoid:
- desperation
- exaggerated hype
- fake luxury
- loud hard sell
- cheesy inspiration

Dubery should feel affordable-premium, not bargain-bin.

---

## Product Framing Rules

Dubery is not sold like a technical gadget.
Do not over-focus on feature jargon.

Better framing:
- easier on the eyes
- bagay sa fit
- ready for the sun
- good for everyday wear
- looks premium
- easy to order today

Worse framing:
- corporate feature dumping
- technical spec language
- "experience advanced lens technology"
- anything sounding like a generic ad agency

## Angle-Specific Voice Notes

### Pain Relief
Tone:
- practical
- immediate
- relatable

Use:
- glare
- squinting
- eye strain
- harsh sun

Avoid making it sound medical.

### Identity
Tone:
- familiar
- validating
- socially accurate

Use:
- "for people who…"
- "if you're the type who…"

Avoid cringe identity labels.

### Lifestyle
Tone:
- visual
- easy
- aspirational but grounded

Use:
- rides
- errands
- weekends
- city movement
- casual outdoor life

### Status / Glow Up
Tone:
- composed
- stylish
- low-key confident

Use:
- looks expensive
- sharp fit
- clean finish

Avoid sounding arrogant.

### Value / Deal
Tone:
- smart
- sharp
- practical

Use:
- "₱699"
- sulit
- smart buy
- premium feel at accessible price

Avoid sounding cheap.

### Convenience / Fast Delivery
Tone:
- immediate
- easy
- frictionless

Use:
- same-day
- next-day
- Metro Manila
- order today, wear today

Avoid repetitive courier wording.

## Hook Guidance

Hook types should vary.

Good hook behaviors:
- question
- POV
- identity statement
- pain point
- confidence line
- speed line
- price surprise
- simple bold statement

A good hook should:
- be understood instantly
- create curiosity or recognition
- set up one clear message

Avoid weak hooks like:
- generic greetings
- vague lifestyle lines
- filler intros
- repeated "looking for…" openings

---

## CTA Guidance

CTA should feel clean and natural.

Preferred CTA style:
- direct
- short
- low-friction

Examples of CTA style:
- DM us
- Message us
- Order now
- Order na ngayon

Do not make CTA sound needy or spammy.

---

## What To Avoid Completely

- corporate tone
- press release energy
- hard-sell agency language
- overly long captions
- trying too hard to sound Gen Z
- heavy Tagalog blocks
- emoji spam
- fake luxury language
- generic product feature dumping
- unnatural bilingual switching
- identical hook patterns across the batch

---

## Final Writing Standard

A strong Dubery caption should feel like:

- instantly readable
- socially native
- Filipino without overdoing it
- stylish without sounding expensive
- persuasive without sounding pushy
- distinct from the other captions in the batch

Every caption should feel like a real idea.

Not a rewrite.
Not a template.
Not filler.
