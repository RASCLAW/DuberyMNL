2. New Format Patterns (Competitive Scan)

Ranked by fit for DuberyMNL voice × production cost × conversion lift. Top 5 carry the orange accent.

✕ GLARE_POV Rejected

Split-frame A/B of same scene through non-polarized vs. polarized lenses.

Why it works
One-frame proof. Viewer feels the benefit without copy.
Rejected session 122 — doesn't fit DuberyMNL's quiet-confidence direction.

#2 TESTIMONIAL_SCREENSHOT High Fit

Real Messenger/FB comment or 5-star review rendered large over product shot. Rough screengrab look — not overly designed.

Why it works
4× CTR vs. branded creative. Reads as peer voice, not ad voice.
Seen at: DIFF Eyewear. We already have real Messenger chats — source material is live.

#3 PRICE_VS_LUXURY High Fit

Side-by-side: "P599 Dubery" vs. "P4,999 Ray-Ban" with similar-looking frames. Or "Same polarized tech. 1/10th the price."

Why it works
Resolves "cheap = low quality" objection head-on. Sulit-culture hook.
Seen at: Knockaround. Directly answers the P599 positioning objection.

#4 PRODUCT_NAMED_POSTER High Fit

Product hero + oversized playful variant name as headline ("Tita-Proof Shades", "Jeepney Cruiser", "Labas-Lang Black"). Colored blocky background.

Why it works
Gives each SKU a personality. Copy does the entertainment.
Seen at: Goodr. Taglish humor plays natively. Can be a content engine by itself.

#5 ACTIVE_LIFESTYLE High Fit

Real people in motion — surfer, cyclist, golfer, runner, hiker, coastal driver, beach volleyball. Product as confident accessory in actual use.

Why it works
Aspirational but attainable. Positions product as gear for the good life, not just fashion.
Seen at: Oakley, Knockaround sport categories. Shift from gritty worker-contexts → weekend-warrior / leisure-active.

#6 UNBOXING_POV Caution

Hands-and-table first-person shot: Dubery pouch/box opening, glasses revealed, mirror check, street step-out. Single image or 3-panel.

Why it works
Reduces purchase anxiety. Legitimizes delivery promise.
Seen at: Warby Parker (#WarbyHomeTryOn), Knockaround (#KnockFan). ⚠ UGC_UNBOXING was dropped earlier — branding over-emphasis broke the temple badge. Revisit as product-focused, no branded-box beauty shots.

#7 PAIN_STATE_HOOK Med-High Fit

Frame 1 = problem (squinting, eye strain, cheap shades breaking, glare headache). Frame 2 = product as the fix.

Why it works
Hooks scroll by agitating felt pain before selling. Top DTC hook structure.
Seen at: Motion DTC hooks 2025, Zeely. "Sakit ng mata sa init" / "Nabasag na naman tingi-tinging shades" works in PH context.

#8 BUNDLE_DEAL_CARD Med-High Fit

Flatlay/exploded: sunglasses + pouch + cloth + case, with per-item values stacked. "Worth P899, yours for P599."

Why it works
Raises perceived value without discounting the core SKU. Justifies the price.
Seen at: Knockaround limited editions, Sunski bundle framing. Pairs naturally with DUBERY50 landing page discount code.

#9 TEXT_REFLECTION Medium Fit

Tight zoom into lens reflection where a phrase or price is printed ("P599", "POLARIZED", "BUY 1 TAKE ½").

Why it works
Unexpected visual trick. Built-in mystery, thumb-stopping.
Seen at: Motion DTC hooks 2025. Cheap to produce — phone + printed card. Reflection fidelity is the risk with Gemini.

#10 OUTFIT_PAIRING_GRID Medium Fit

Same pair styled across 3–4 looks: beach fit / office casual / Friday gimmick / Sunday mass. Grid or carousel.

Why it works
Reframes sunglasses as wardrobe, not accessory. Multi-occasion = more perceived use.
Seen at: Zeely outfit carousels, Warby Parker lifestyle.

6. Knockaround-Inspired Playbook

After reviewing knockaround.com, /collections, and /pages/shop-instagram directly, this is the direction DuberyMNL should lean into. Limited SKU catalog (~11) is not a blocker — Knockaround's patterns work at any scale.

6.1 The Core Principle: Photography-First

Knockaround's collection tiles have zero text overlays. Every tile is pure photography — real people, real moments, natural light. The label lives below the image with a simple arrow →, not on top of it.

This flips our current brand-content mix (BOLD typography sandwich / CALLOUT feature labels / COLLECTION series headers) where text dominates. For discovery + browsing surfaces, photography-first wins because the vibe sells before the copy does.

The rule:

  • Discovery / browsing / social feed → no text on image
  • Ads / promos / feature explainers → typography still OK
  • Ratio target: 60% photography-first, 40% typography-driven

knockaround.com/collections — tiles are photography-only, labels below with →

6.2 Shop Our Instagram Wall

knockaround.com/pages/shop-instagram — shoppable IG grid

The genius: a page on duberymnl.com that pulls real Instagram posts, arranged as a square grid, each tile clickable to the exact SKU worn. Small shopping-bag icon in the corner of each image signals "this is shoppable."

Why it works for DuberyMNL with 11 SKUs:

  • Social proof at scale without manual testimonial production
  • Turns your IG feed into direct funnel — every post does double duty
  • Compounds over time: more posts = richer wall
  • Makes strangers look like friends wearing your product
  • Zero ongoing production cost once wired

Build path: Shopify apps like Foursixty, Curator.io, or Shoppable-Instagram automate this (IG Graph API → shoppable grid with SKU tagging). Or roll custom: IG Graph API + simple grid + product-tag JSON.

Bootstrap problem: You need IG content first. Solution → new content category UGC_STYLE (below) fills the wall until real customer posts arrive.

6.3 New Brand Content Category: LIFESTYLE_TILE + UGC_STYLE

NEW LIFESTYLE_TILE Photography-first

Pure photography, 4:5 or 1:1 — product worn naturally in a lifestyle moment. No typography. No logo overlay. Mood and vibe carry the tile.

Placement
duberymnl.com collection tiles, FB carousel ads (caption-only), Shopee listing covers, landing page hero slices.
Labels live outside the image (post caption, card footer, or arrow-below). The image does the selling.

NEW UGC_STYLE IG-native

Square 1:1, Instagram-aesthetic — looks like a customer post. Natural light, unposed-feeling composition. Zero overlays.

Placement
@duberymnl IG feed + duberymnl.com/shop-instagram wall. Bootstraps the wall until real customer content builds up.
Different from our existing UGC_* v3 pipeline — that's photorealistic for-scene; this is feed-native for-grid. Tighter composition, IG-feel crop, simpler lighting.

6.5 Content Mix Rebalance

Current mix (ad-heavy)

  • ~70% typography-dominant (BOLD, CALLOUT, COLLECTION)
  • ~30% photorealistic scenes (UGC pipeline)
  • 0% pure-photography discovery tiles
  • 0% IG-native feed content

Proposed mix (Knockaround-direction)

  • ~40% LIFESTYLE_TILE + UGC_STYLE — photography-first, no text
  • ~20% MODEL_SHOT — clean portraits on solid color
  • ~25% typography formats (BOLD, CALLOUT, COLLECTION) — reserved for ads/promos
  • ~15% new specialty formats (GLARE_POV, TESTIMONIAL, PRICE_VS_LUXURY, NAMED_POSTER)

6.6 What to Drop from Current Site

  • Heavy text overlays on hero/tiles (photography-first replaces this)
  • Catalog-grid product thumbnails (swap for lifestyle-tile photography)
  • Aspirational-without-substance headlines (replace with Quiet Confidence bank or drop entirely)
  • Any remaining commuter / gritty imagery (already removed from randomizer banks)

④ Shop-IG Wall (Path A — no Shopify)

Three ways to wire the IG grid without Shopify:

Curator.io

Embed-code widget. Works on any static site. ~$35-95/mo.

Cheapest, fastest, no dev. Not shoppable out-of-box but supports click-through to product URLs via tags.

Elfsight Instagram Feed

Embed widget. Free tier exists. Paid ~$5-15/mo for real-time.

Simple grid, no shopping integration — visitors click through to IG post, not product.

Custom (IG Graph API + JSON map)

Fetch recent posts via Instagram Basic Display API. Map each ig_post_id → product_key in a JSON file. Grid + overlay link.

Free but needs dev time (~4-6 hrs). Full control over look, can link directly to Messenger order flow instead of cart.
